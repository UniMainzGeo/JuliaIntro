Highlights

Real-world problems

We will take applications such as climate change and show how you can participate in the big open source community looking to find solutions to challenging problems with exposure to github and parallel computing.

Corgi in the washing machine

You will learn mathematical ideas by immersion into the mathematical process, performing experiments, seeing the connections, and seeing just how much fun math can be.

Revolutionary interactivity

Our course material is built using real code, and instead of a book, we have a series of interactive notebooks. On our website, you can play with sliders, buttons and images to interact with our simulations. You can even go further, and modify and run any code on our website!

Why is this course different?

We believe many classes cover what we call the vertices ‚Äî specific topics in computer science, math, or an application. A student is left to figure out the edges, meaning the intellectual connections between the topics on their own as they mature. Some classes have you learn a math topic and you can then for homework implement an algorithm or application. The goal for this class, is to accelerate the process by which a student can participate in the exciting world of software development be it the big open source universe or privately, by seeing how math with CS abstractions can allow for applications that can be part of a big huge ecosystem rather than a one-off homework.
